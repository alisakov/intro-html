Self Care Tips

Recommended By Alisa K.

You are not alone. Panic attacks are more common than you think - especially as a result of the effects of the Covid-19. Take a minute to read what helped me to get through hard times and don't forget to breath.

  • Breathing Exercise

    • Breathing is the first step to calm your body. when your body is calm, your mind calms itself automatically after a while because it no more feels in danger (fight or fight affect).

  • Wysa App

    • It's always best that when you're having an anxiety attack to find a person around you feel close enough to, to spill your feelings to. Even if they wouldn't be able to help, the act of talking your feelings out will help.

    but in case you are around strangers and you can't call your loved ones, there is this adorable and soothing app - a penguin AI therapist. it includes psychology insights and exercises. Very useful in moments of need and with getting out of your head.

  • Music

    • I recommend putting together a playlist of songs that calm you down. It can be songs that are quiet and soothing or ones that cheer you up. When you feel you're getting anxious, and talking to people dindn't help or you just don't feel like it, find a quiet place away from people, put on your headphones and listen to that playlist you made earlier.
